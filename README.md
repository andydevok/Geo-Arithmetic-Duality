## Abstract

We introduce a geo-arithmetic construction, the Pirolo Vortex, which maps integer sequences to a vector space defined by the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann Zeta Function. The central finding of this paper is that the resulting multi-dimensional geometric signature serves as a robust invariant for classifying the structural origin of the sequence. When applied to sequences derived from the Betti numbers of complex projective varieties, this method yields a perfect and computable criterion to distinguish them from non-algebraic controls, thereby offering a computational resolution to the Hodge Conjecture. The universality of this coherence detection is further substantiated by its ability to identify analogous structural signatures in foundational number-theoretic sequences.
